# Practice Exam 03 - Full Semester

Course: [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Course Overview|Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)]]
Coverage: Weeks 01-12
Suggested timing: 2 hours, closed book
Marks: 100

## Instructions

Answer all questions. Prioritise structured judgement over memorised lists.

## Section A - Short Answers

Each question is worth 2 marks.

1. Why is ambiguity normal in consulting work?
2. What professional behaviours help build client trust?
3. How does a trusted advisor relationship differ from an expert-for-hire relationship?
4. What should pricing and assumptions tell the client?
5. Why should consultants keep a private work record?
6. What is the risk of avoiding difficult messages?
7. Why should advice show trade-offs?
8. How can objective criteria improve negotiation?
9. What is the difference between a finding and a recommendation?
10. Why should problem-solving criteria be selected before alternatives are favoured?

## Section B - Applied Questions

Each question is worth 8 marks.

11. A consultant joins a client meeting late, unprepared, and with generic slides. Explain how this affects trust.
12. A client has a preferred answer before analysis starts. Explain how hypothesis-driven consulting can help without becoming client capture.
13. A report introduces major new evidence in the conclusion. Explain why this is a report-writing problem.
14. A meeting has no clear decision, too many attendees, and no follow-up actions. Explain how to improve it.
15. A technically strong recommendation is outside the client's budget and implementation capability. Explain why it is weak advice.

## Section C - Scenario Analysis

Each question is worth 20 marks.

16. A health services organisation asks consultants to recommend whether to centralise patient intake. Some clinicians oppose the idea, administrators want efficiency, executives want risk reduction, and patients experience inconsistent service.

Explain how consultants should frame the problem, develop and test hypotheses, gather evidence, manage stakeholders, and communicate a recommendation.

17. A consulting engagement is entering final close-out. The client accepts most findings but disputes the roadmap, asks for extra implementation planning, and wants the consultants to present to a broader leadership group.

Explain how the consulting team should manage scope, negotiation, final presentation, sign-off, residual risks, handover, and future relationship.
