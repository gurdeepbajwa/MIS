# Milestone Exam 03 - Reports Workshops Hypotheses and Close Out

Course: [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Course Overview|Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)]]
Coverage: [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Week 07/Lecture|Week 07]], [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Week 08/Lecture|Week 08]], [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Week 09/Lecture|Week 09]], [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Week 10/Lecture|Week 10]], [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Week 11/Lecture|Week 11]]
Suggested timing: 90 minutes, closed book
Marks: 50

## Scope

This milestone exam checks reports, workshops, meetings, interviews, hypothesis-driven consulting, problem solving, difficult conversations, and engagement close-out.

## Section A - Short Answers

Each question is worth 2 marks.

1. Why should a consulting report answer the client's question rather than record activities completed?
2. What should an approach section in a consulting report explain?
3. When is a workshop more useful than a meeting or interview?
4. What makes a consulting meeting worth the client's time?
5. Distinguish a meeting from an interview and a workshop.
6. Why should interview notes distinguish observations, stakeholder opinions, consultant interpretations, and follow-up evidence needs?
7. What makes a hypothesis testable?
8. Why is hypothesis-driven consulting not the same as assuming the answer?
9. Name the six steps in the IESE problem-solving process.
10. What makes engagement close-out successful?

## Section B - Applied Questions

Each question is worth 5 marks.

11. A report spends most of its pages describing meetings held and documents reviewed. Explain what is weak and how to improve it.
12. A workshop reveals a different problem from the one assumed in the proposal. Explain how the consultant should handle this.
13. A consultant becomes attached to an early hypothesis even after evidence weakens it. Explain the risk.
14. A client stakeholder resists interviews and appears defensive. Explain how the consultant should interpret and manage this behaviour.

## Section C - Integrated Scenario

Each question is worth 10 marks.

15. A retail client has grown by acquisition and now has fragmented systems, inconsistent processes, and distrust between business and IT. The board wants a report recommending a path forward.

Explain how consultants should gather evidence through meetings, interviews, and workshops; use hypothesis-driven analysis; structure the report; and make recommendations that are useful after the consultants leave.

16. Near the end of an engagement, the sponsor disputes a finding, a department head asks for extra analysis outside scope, and unresolved risks remain. Explain how the consultant should manage conflict, governance, close-out, handover, sign-off, and relationship preservation.
