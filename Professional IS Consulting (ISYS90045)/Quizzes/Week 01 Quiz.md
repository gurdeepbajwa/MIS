## Quiz 1

### Note-Based Questions

1. Why do organisations commonly hire consultants?
   - A. To avoid making any internal decisions.
   - B. To access expertise, external perspective, temporary capacity, credibility, or structured delivery methods.
   - C. To replace all internal project staff.
   - D. To remove the need for stakeholder communication.

2. Why is IS consulting broader than technical advice?

3. Which type of consulting firm is most closely connected to products, platforms, or implementation ecosystems?
   - A. Vendor consultancy.
   - B. Internal consultancy.
   - C. Niche consultancy.
   - D. Partnership firm.

4. What is one strength and one limitation of internal consultants?

5. Which statement best explains why reputation and professional networks matter in consulting?
   - A. They replace the need for evidence.
   - B. They matter because clients buy trust before they buy advice.
   - C. They remove commercial risk.
   - D. They make formal communication unnecessary.

6. Name three consulting skills or capabilities beyond subject matter expertise.

7. Why is ambiguity a normal part of consulting work?

8. Which statement best describes a risk of vendor consulting?
   - A. Vendor consultants cannot understand products deeply.
   - B. Vendor consultants are always more independent than other external consultants.
   - C. Vendor advice may be shaped by the product or platform the vendor sells.
   - D. Vendor consulting only applies to legal work.

9. Give one example of how professional conduct can affect client trust.

10. A client says they need a new system. What other consulting issues might need to be diagnosed before recommending a solution?

### Extension Questions

11. Professional consulting codes often emphasise competence, confidentiality, independence, conflicts of interest, and client interests. How does this extend the Week 01 point that clients buy trust before they buy advice?

12. Which professional risk is most directly raised when a consultant recommends a vendor's own platform without clearly acknowledging the commercial relationship?
   - A. A conflict of interest or perceived loss of independence.
   - B. Too much professional development.
   - C. Excessive client confidentiality.
   - D. Too much stakeholder access.

13. AI is increasingly discussed as changing the traditional consulting pyramid by automating junior research, analysis, and drafting tasks. How does this strengthen the Week 01 note's emphasis on judgement, synthesis, and client-facing capability?

14. Give one example of a consulting situation where a technically correct recommendation may still fail because of weak professional conduct or poor stakeholder trust.

15. Which statement best extends the Week 01 notes into professional practice?
   - A. Consulting value comes only from having more technical knowledge than the client.
   - B. Consulting value comes from turning expertise into trusted, evidence-based advice that fits the client's problem, context, and stakeholders.
   - C. Consulting value comes from avoiding ambiguity.
   - D. Consulting value comes from always recommending the newest technology.
