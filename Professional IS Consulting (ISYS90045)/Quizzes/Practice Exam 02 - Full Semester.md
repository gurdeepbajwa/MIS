# Practice Exam 02 - Full Semester

Course: [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Course Overview|Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)]]
Coverage: Weeks 01-12
Suggested timing: 2 hours, closed book
Marks: 100

## Instructions

Answer all questions. Strong answers should connect trust, evidence, stakeholder needs, risk, recommendation, and communication.

## Section A - Short Answers

Each question is worth 2 marks.

1. What is one strength and one limitation of internal consultants?
2. Why can vendor advice be useful and risky at the same time?
3. What does "understanding of needs" show in a proposal?
4. Why can a weak proposal create later delivery problems?
5. What does scope change require in a consulting engagement?
6. How can low self-orientation be shown in a client presentation?
7. What makes advice actionable?
8. Why should negotiation preparation include a bottom line?
9. Why should interview notes be written up promptly?
10. Why is close-out a relationship-management moment?

## Section B - Applied Questions

Each question is worth 8 marks.

11. A client asks for "more detail" in a proposal, but the procurement rules require equal information access for all bidders. Explain how the consultant should respond.
12. A consultant uses text-heavy slides and reads from a script while executives lose attention. Explain the communication issues.
13. A client wants the consultant to recommend a decision but the evidence is incomplete. Explain how the advice should be framed.
14. A workshop contains both senior managers and junior staff, and junior staff stop contributing. Explain the facilitation risk and alternative design.
15. A consultant's first hypothesis is disproved by interview and system evidence. Explain how this should be communicated to the client.

## Section C - Scenario Analysis

Each question is worth 20 marks.

16. A charity engages consultants to review donor-management processes. Executives want strategic recommendations, frontline staff want practical fixes, IT wants technical clarity, and donors' privacy must be protected.

Explain how consultants should manage audience-aware communication, evidence gathering, advice quality, reporting, and trust throughout the engagement.

17. A sponsor asks the consulting team to deliver the final report without mentioning unresolved governance risks because the board meeting is approaching.

Explain how the consultant should handle the difficult conversation, protect advice quality, manage conflict, and close out the engagement responsibly.
