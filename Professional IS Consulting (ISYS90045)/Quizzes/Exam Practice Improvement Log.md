# PISC Exam Practice Improvement Log

- 2026-06-02: Proposals - include the standard proposal contents: client problem, services, methodology/timeline, pricing, assumptions, team, and similar-work evidence.
- 2026-06-02: Proposals - emphasise both client confidence/value/risk and consultant deliverability through scope, price, timeline, and profitability.
- 2026-06-02: Proposal request types - distinguish RFI as market information, EOI as testing supplier interest, and RFQ as pricing for standardised services.
- 2026-06-02: Audience analysis - apply proposal sections to scenario facts, not just say "tailor it"; name decision makers, technical evaluators, procurement, and impacted users.
- 2026-06-02: Proposal scenarios - include concrete risks such as business disruption, data migration, training, user adoption, assumptions, and success measures.
- 2026-06-02: Bid/no-bid judgement - apply all tender criteria: capability, mandatory compliance, references, insurance, pricing rules, presentation stages, win probability, and delivery risk.
- 2026-06-02: Proposal structure - keep the executive summary decision-focused; define the introduction as background, scope, structure, and consultant fit, not the technical-detail section.
- 2026-06-02: Pricing and assumptions - name concrete limits and exclusions, such as number of workshops, testing scope, training sessions, support hours, client inputs, timelines, and change-control rules.
- 2026-06-02: Understanding of needs - interpret the client's real operational issue instead of repeating the RFP; include disruption, adoption, data migration, business impact, and success measures.
- 2026-06-02: Audience analysis - cover every named evaluator: senior leadership, technical evaluators, procurement, and operational stakeholders, with their distinct concerns.
- 2026-06-02: Formal procurement - use the official clarification channel; in government RFTs, questions and answers may be shared with all bidders.
- 2026-06-02: Client relationships - use Sobel's distinction between vendor/expert-for-hire and trusted advisor/partner; large revenue is still vulnerable if the relationship lacks agenda-setting, relationship capital, and many-to-many trust.
- 2026-06-02: Low-price proposals - connect underpricing to discovery, testing, training, senior resourcing, quality, scope creep, change requests, profitability, and trust.
- 2026-06-02: Proposal commitments - avoid unlimited workshops/support; define scope, time limits, deliverables, assumptions, governance, change control, and close-out criteria.
