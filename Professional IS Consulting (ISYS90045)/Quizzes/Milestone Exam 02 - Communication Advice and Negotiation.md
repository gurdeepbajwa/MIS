# Milestone Exam 02 - Communication Advice and Negotiation

Course: [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Course Overview|Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)]]
Coverage: [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Week 04/Lecture|Week 04]], [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Week 05/Lecture|Week 05]], [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Week 06/Lecture|Week 06]]
Suggested timing: 75 minutes, closed book
Marks: 45

## Scope

This milestone exam checks professional communication, trust, presentation, advice quality, advice delivery, negotiation preparation, negotiation styles, and relationship-preserving negotiation.

## Section A - Short Answers

Each question is worth 2 marks.

1. Why must consulting communication be audience-aware?
2. Distinguish information, analysis, and advice.
3. Name the four elements of the trusted advisor trust model.
4. Explain what low self-orientation means in consulting.
5. Why can overstating certainty damage credibility?
6. What makes professional advice different from personal opinion?
7. Why should advice make assumptions, limits, risks, and dependencies visible?
8. Why is negotiation a recurring consulting skill rather than only a contract activity?
9. Distinguish open-book negotiation from take-it-or-leave-it negotiation.
10. Explain when saying no can strengthen trust.

## Section B - Applied Questions

Each question is worth 5 marks.

11. A consultant presents a highly technical recommendation to executives without explaining business implications. Explain the communication failure.
12. A consultant gives the client ten pages of facts but no recommendation. Explain why this is weak advice.
13. A client asks for a lower fixed fee while keeping scope, timeline, and quality unchanged. Explain how the consultant should negotiate.
14. A consultant accepts an unrealistic timeline to win work. Explain the likely consequences for trust, delivery, and relationship.
15. A client challenges the consultant's evidence during a presentation. Explain how the consultant should respond without becoming defensive.

## Section C - Scenario

This question is worth 10 marks.

16. A consulting team has found that a client's preferred system upgrade will not fix the underlying process and data-quality problems. The sponsor is politically committed to the upgrade and wants the recommendation softened before the steering committee.

Explain how the consultant should communicate the advice, manage trust, show evidence, handle uncertainty, and negotiate the discussion without abandoning professional judgement.
