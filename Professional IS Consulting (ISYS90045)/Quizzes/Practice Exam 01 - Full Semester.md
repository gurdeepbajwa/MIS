# Practice Exam 01 - Full Semester

Course: [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Course Overview|Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)]]
Coverage: Weeks 01-12
Suggested timing: 2 hours, closed book
Marks: 100

## Instructions

Answer all questions. Use consulting language precisely and apply frameworks to the client context.

## Section A - Short Answers

Each question is worth 2 marks.

1. Why do clients buy trust before they buy advice?
2. Why is technical knowledge alone insufficient for consulting work?
3. Distinguish an executive summary from an introduction.
4. Explain when a consultancy should decide not to bid.
5. Name the phases of the engagement life cycle.
6. What are credibility, reliability, intimacy, and low self-orientation?
7. Distinguish information, analysis, and advice.
8. Why is negotiation also a delivery skill?
9. What makes a consulting report useful after the consultant leaves?
10. What makes a hypothesis testable?

## Section B - Applied Questions

Each question is worth 8 marks.

11. A vendor consultant recommends their own platform without acknowledging the commercial relationship. Explain the professional risk.
12. A client asks for extra workshops without extra budget. Explain how a consultant should handle the request.
13. A consultant presents recommendations without explaining evidence or trade-offs. Explain why the advice may fail.
14. A steering meeting becomes a long activity diary. Explain how to redesign it into a useful consulting meeting.
15. A client resists a finding because it is politically uncomfortable. Explain how the consultant should manage the conversation.

## Section C - Scenario Analysis

Each question is worth 20 marks.

16. A regional supermarket chain has duplicated systems, inconsistent store processes, poor inventory visibility, and distrust between business and IT. The board wants advice on whether to start an enterprise architecture initiative.

Write an exam-style consulting answer explaining how the consulting team should diagnose the problem, gather evidence, structure workshops and interviews, use hypothesis-driven analysis, and present advice through a report.

17. A consultancy is invited to bid for a digital transformation review. The client wants a low fixed price, fast delivery, and broad scope. The consulting team has relevant experience but limited staff availability during the proposed period.

Explain the bid/no-bid judgement, proposal assumptions, negotiation issues, scope risks, and engagement controls the consultancy should consider.
