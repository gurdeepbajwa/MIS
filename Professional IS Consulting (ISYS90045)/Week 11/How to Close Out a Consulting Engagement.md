# How to Close Out a Consulting Engagement

Course: [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Course Overview|Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)]]
Week: 11
Source: [[Professional IS Consulting (ISYS90045)/Week 11/Lecture|Week 11 Lecture]]
Diataxis type: How-to guide

## Purpose

Use this guide when a consulting scenario asks how to finish, hand over, sign off, or learn from an engagement.

## Steps

1. Confirm completion status.
   - Check whether scope, deliverables, acceptance criteria, and expected outcomes have been met.
2. Review with the right people.
   - Include consulting leaders, client sponsors, project governance, legal or risk reviewers, and user representatives where relevant.
3. Collect sign-off.
   - Document internal approval and client acceptance in writing.
   - Include user acceptance when the client sponsor is not the end user.
4. Handle feedback and final changes.
   - Decide what can be fixed before closure and what requires change control, maintenance, or a new contract.
5. Handover ownership.
   - Transfer documentation, licences, product ownership, support arrangements, open issues, and residual risks.
6. Close the project account.
   - Disband the project team and stop further charging to the closed engagement.
7. Record lessons learned.
   - Capture success, failure, unresolved problems, and patterns the consulting firm should learn from.

## Close-Out Evidence

| Evidence | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- |
| Signed acceptance | Shows the client received and accepted the deliverables. |
| User acceptance | Shows the solution works for the people expected to use it. |
| Issue and risk register | Makes unresolved work visible. |
| Handover pack | Allows support or maintenance teams to continue the work. |
| End-of-project report | Records performance against scope, schedule, cost, quality, and benefits. |
| Lessons learned report | Helps the consulting firm improve future engagements. |

## Exam Use

A strong exam answer should explain:

- why formal closure is needed;
- who needs to sign off;
- what evidence should be documented;
- how unresolved issues should be handled;
- how closure protects the client relationship and the consulting firm.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating close-out as just a final presentation.
- Assuming sponsor approval is enough when users also need to accept the solution.
- Accepting extra work after sign-off without checking scope or contract terms.
- Failing to document partial failure or unresolved risks.
- Losing lessons that could improve future consulting work.
