## Case Study Options

### Harper Fitness

Harper Fitness is a global gym and wellness chain seeking a technology refresh. Its website is outdated, it lacks a comprehensive mobile app, class bookings are mostly manual, and member, marketing and operational data are siloed.

Proposed initiatives:

- Website refresh with modern design and clearer user journeys.
- Mobile app for class bookings, membership management and wearable integration.
- 24/7 chatbot for member support.
- Online wellness services, including digital coaching, class streaming and nutrition tracking.
- Better digital engagement for 18-30-year-old members.

Key proposal angle: modernise member-facing digital channels to improve customer experience, satisfaction, membership uptake and retention.

Likely difficulty: easiest. The problem, solution, benefits, timeline and risks are clear and manageable.

### BrightMart

BrightMart is a mid-sized supermarket and convenience retail chain facing rising grocery costs, stronger digital competitors and outdated systems. Its digital presence is limited, and leadership must balance digital transformation with affordability, supplier fairness and community values.

Proposed initiatives:

- Redesigned website with e-commerce and delivery integration.
- Mobile app for shopping, order tracking and loyalty rewards.
- Chatbot for customer enquiries.
- Real-time stock and pricing integration.
- Improved click-and-collect and later full delivery capability.

Key proposal angle: refresh BrightMart's digital retail capabilities while protecting customer trust, affordability and supplier relationships.

Likely difficulty: moderate. The technology refresh is clear, but the external grocery-cost context and stakeholder tensions add complexity.

### Austech Manufacturing Alliance

Austech Manufacturing Alliance is a network of nine manufacturing plants seeking a unified ERP system. Each plant currently uses different finance, HR, supply chain, production and inventory systems. The case also asks the consultancy to consider energy usage, emissions reporting and electricity price volatility.

Proposed initiatives:

- ERP discovery across all nine plants.
- Cloud versus on-premises ERP analysis.
- Vendor selection support, such as SAP, Oracle or Microsoft Dynamics.
- Roadmap for finance, HR, supply chain, production, maintenance and energy data.
- Change management, training, rollout, testing and support.

Key proposal angle: standardise fragmented operations through a phased ERP implementation while managing multi-site change, production disruption and energy-data complexity.

Likely difficulty: hardest. The ERP scope, multi-site rollout, stakeholder resistance, energy-market considerations and data integration risks make this the most complex option.

## Project Management Guidance

The proposal should show awareness of the main project management dimensions:

- **Scope:** define what is included and excluded.
- **Approach:** justify the delivery methodology.
- **Deliverables:** separate internal and external outputs.
- **Time:** include phases, milestones and a Gantt-style timeline.
- **Resources:** explain roles, skills and staffing requirements.
- **Risk:** identify, assess, prioritise and mitigate risks.
- **Governance:** define decision-making, reporting and escalation.

For Harper Fitness or BrightMart, a **hybrid Agile approach** is likely strongest:

- Use upfront discovery and planning to confirm scope, business requirements, integrations, budget and governance.
- Use iterative design, build and testing for the website, app, chatbot and customer-facing functionality.
- Use staged releases so the client can review working outputs and provide feedback.
- Keep formal controls around scope, cost, security, data privacy and go-live readiness.

For AMA, use a more structured phased or hybrid ERP approach because data migration, process standardisation, plant-level disruption and stakeholder resistance create higher implementation risk.

## Risk Management Guidance

Use a clear risk management method such as **ISO 31000**:

1. Identify risks.
2. Assess probability and impact.
3. Prioritise risks using a risk matrix.
4. Define mitigation actions.
5. Assign risk ownership.
6. Monitor and update risks throughout the project.

Risk categories to consider:

- Scope creep
- Budget overrun
- Timeline delay
- Integration failure
- Data migration issues
- Cybersecurity and privacy
- Stakeholder resistance
- Operational disruption during rollout
- Poor adoption or insufficient training

For each major risk, explain:

- Probability
- Impact on cost, time or quality
- Mitigation
- Owner
- Escalation path

The most important risk should be highlighted clearly for the client, with a practical explanation of what the consultancy will do to reduce it.

## Governance Guidance

The governance section should explain how the project will be controlled, how decisions will be made and how issues will be escalated.

Suggested governance model:

- **Programme Steering Committee:** monthly strategic forum for sponsors, stream leads, partners and directors. Reviews budget, timeline, key deliverables, major risks, issues, scope and strategic decisions.
- **Project Management Forum:** weekly tactical forum for the client project manager, consulting directors and managers. Reviews progress, dependencies, risks, issues and remediation actions.
- **Stand-ups / Agile Scrums:** daily or several times per week. Used by delivery team members to discuss current work, blockers and backlog priorities.

Suggested consulting roles:

- **Partner:** overall engagement leadership and ownership of scope of services.
- **Director:** project lead and subject matter oversight across streams.
- **Manager:** day-to-day project management and stream leadership.
- **Senior Analysts / Analysts:** delivery team members who complete analysis, documentation, testing and implementation support.

Key governance question to answer: how will issues be managed and by whom if budget, timeline or scope goes off track?
