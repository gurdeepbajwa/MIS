## When We Start Exam Prep

Prioritise scenario application over memorising definitions.

For each practice answer:

- name the relevant concept or framework;
- define it briefly only if needed;
- apply it to the facts in the scenario;
- cite the specific evidence that makes it fit;
- explain the implication for the consultant or client;
- give a practical recommendation or next step.

## Week 11 Warning

Do not stop at labels.

For example, if the question asks about conflict:

- identify whether it is intragroup or intergroup conflict;
- prove the label using case evidence;
- identify likely causes such as fear, workload, job-loss concern, poor timing, lack of trust, or unclear communication;
- propose a response that fits the consultant's authority;
- explain how escalation, negotiation, or close-out documentation would reduce risk.

## High-Value Exam Habits

- Treat the exam as open-book but prepare as if it is closed-book.
- Organise hard-copy notes by topic and framework so they are fast to retrieve.
- Attempt every question.
- Use bullet points when they help structure the answer, but make the reasoning explicit.
- For any method question, do not just list steps. Explain how the steps change what the consultant should do in the case.
- For any close-out question, mention sign-off, handover, user acceptance, unresolved issues, residual risks, and lessons learned where relevant.
