# How to Build a Future State and Gap Analysis

Course: [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Course Overview|Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)]]
Week: 09
Source: [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Week 09/Lecture|Week 09 Lecture]]
Diataxis type: How-to guide

## Purpose

Use future state and gap analysis to define what the business needs to become before choosing a detailed solution.

## When to Use It

Use this guide when a scenario asks for:

- future state analysis;
- business goals or SMART objectives;
- solution scope;
- gap analysis;
- change strategy;
- readiness or transition planning.

## Steps

1. Start with the business need.
2. Translate the need into a high-level business requirement.
3. Define business goals that describe the intended improvement.
4. Convert goals into SMART objectives where measurement is possible.
5. Describe the future state using compatible categories, such as POPIT.
6. Define solution scope by identifying what must change, not the final solution vendor or product.
7. Compare current state and future state to identify gaps.
8. Assign each gap to a responsible business area where possible.
9. Identify actions that could close each gap.
10. Assess readiness, constraints, risks, and transition states.
11. Recommend a change strategy that fits size, risk, complexity, and value-realisation pattern.

## Gap Analysis Table

| Current State | Future State | Gap | Example Evidence | Responsible Area | Possible Action | Size |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| What exists now. | What should exist. | Difference between current and future. | Data, elicitation, observation, document evidence. | Business unit or owner. | Process, people, organisation, information, or technology action. | Small, medium, or large. |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Better Practice |
| --- | --- |
| Describing the future state as a specific tool | Define the desired capability or outcome first. |
| Writing vague goals such as "improve service" | Convert goals into measurable SMART objectives where possible. |
| Comparing current and future state using different categories | Use compatible structures, such as POPIT, to make gaps visible. |
| Ignoring readiness | Assess skills, stakeholder support, training, capacity, and change champions. |
| Treating the change strategy as implementation detail only | Link the strategy to value, risk, readiness, transition, and constraints. |

## Exam Use

A strong exam answer should show the chain:

Business need -> business requirement -> goals -> SMART objectives -> future state -> solution scope -> gap analysis -> change strategy -> readiness and risk.

Use this chain to avoid jumping from problem to technology.

## Retrieval Prompts

1. What is the difference between future state and solution scope?
2. Why should future state avoid naming the final solution too early?
3. How does POPIT help gap analysis?
4. What readiness factors can cause a good solution to fail?
