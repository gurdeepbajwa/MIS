# Practice Exam 02 - Full Semester

Course: [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Course Overview|Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)]]
Coverage: Weeks 01-12
Suggested timing: 2 hours, closed book
Marks: 100

## Instructions

Answer all questions. Strong answers should show distinctions, evidence needs, and business value reasoning.

## Section A - Short Answers

Each question is worth 2 marks.

1. Explain why digital technology should not be treated as the solution by default.
2. Distinguish a task from a technique in BA work.
3. What is the purpose of a change control process?
4. Why should context analysis be purpose-driven?
5. Distinguish scope definition from solution scope.
6. Give two reasons stakeholder attitudes matter in BA work.
7. Distinguish a symptom from a cause.
8. What is requirements traceability?
9. Distinguish verification and validation of requirements.
10. Why can KPIs become harmful if chosen poorly?

## Section B - Applied Questions

Each question is worth 8 marks.

11. A manager wants to skip stakeholder interviews because "we already have the data." Explain why this may weaken analysis.
12. A BA creates personas from assumptions rather than research. Explain the risk and what evidence should be used instead.
13. A project has a clear future state but no transition plan. Explain the missing analysis.
14. A design option has high expected value but also high adoption risk. Explain how the BA should evaluate and present this trade-off.
15. A metric shows average processing time improved, but customers still report poor service. Explain how the BA should analyse this variance.

## Section C - Scenario Analysis

Each question is worth 20 marks.

16. A bank wants to replace manual loan application processing with a digital workflow. Staff say manual work creates delays, compliance says documentation quality is inconsistent, customers say the process is confusing, and executives want measurable efficiency gains.

Explain how a BA should move from problem analysis to requirements and design options. Include stakeholders, elicitation, root cause analysis, requirement types, requirement quality, and value assessment.

17. A local council wants to create a digital permit application service. Political leaders want visible improvement quickly, but users include builders, residents, older citizens, internal assessors, and external regulators.

Explain how the BA should conduct strategy analysis and plan a change strategy that balances value, readiness, constraints, risk, and evaluation.
