## Quiz 1

### Note-Based Questions

1. Which BA planning and monitoring task clarifies who makes project-related decisions and how those decisions are made?
   - A. Plan BA Approach
   - B. Plan BA Governance
   - C. Plan Stakeholder Engagement
   - D. Identify BA Performance Improvements

2. Which planning aspect asks what internal and external aspects affect the business and the analysis work?
   - A. Background
   - B. Scope
   - C. Context
   - D. Approval

3. Why is the business analysis plan described as a living document?

4. Which statement best describes an adaptive approach?
   - A. The solution is fully defined before implementation to maximise control.
   - B. Tasks are performed only in fixed phases.
   - C. Information is gathered through team interaction and feedback, and work is performed iteratively.
   - D. Formal templates are always the main source of information.

5. Explain the difference between a business analysis task and a technique.

6. Which factor is listed as a project complexity factor in BA planning?
   - A. Stakeholder availability
   - B. Experience of the business analyst
   - C. Number of systems affected
   - D. Time or financial restrictions

7. What is the difference between the BA approach and project management, according to the Week 02 note?

8. Which governance element defines how proposed changes are raised, assessed, approved, and managed?
   - A. Decision Making Process
   - B. Change Control Process
   - C. Plan for Approvals
   - D. Plan Prioritisation Approach

9. Why should information management consider privacy, access rights, storage location, and third-party tool reliance?

10. Why does the note warn that performance measures should be chosen carefully?

### Extension Questions

11. IIBA describes Plan Business Analysis Information Management as defining how BA information will be stored, accessed, and used during and after a change. How could weak information management create risk in a large digital transformation program?

12. IIBA's Core Standard connects information management with the BA approach, governance approach, and stakeholder engagement approach. Which answer best explains why these inputs matter?
   - A. Information management can be planned without considering stakeholders or governance.
   - B. Information storage, access, and use must fit the chosen approach, decision rights, and stakeholder information needs.
   - C. Information management is only relevant after the solution has been delivered.
   - D. Governance replaces the need to document information.

13. The IIBA Business Analysis Standard notes that some initiatives use predictive approaches and others use adaptive approaches. In practice, why might a large organisation combine both approaches in the same initiative?

14. Give one example of a BA performance metric that could create bad behaviour if used poorly, and explain the risk.

15. In a project with strict regulatory requirements and high uncertainty about user needs, what planning trade-off would a business analyst need to manage?
