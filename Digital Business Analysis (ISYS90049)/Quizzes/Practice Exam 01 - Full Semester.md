# Practice Exam 01 - Full Semester

Course: [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Course Overview|Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)]]
Coverage: Weeks 01-12
Suggested timing: 2 hours, closed book
Marks: 100

## Instructions

Answer all questions. Use course language precisely and apply concepts to the scenario rather than listing definitions only.

## Section A - Short Answers

Each question is worth 2 marks.

1. Why should a business analyst investigate need, context, stakeholders, value, and solution together?
2. Distinguish BA governance from project management.
3. Why can poor BA performance measures create poor analyst behaviour?
4. Distinguish external context analysis from internal context analysis.
5. What does POPIT help analysts notice?
6. Distinguish stakeholder analysis from stakeholder management planning.
7. Why should elicitation results be confirmed?
8. Why is root cause analysis important before requirements are finalised?
9. Distinguish business requirements from solution requirements.
10. What makes a requirement testable?

## Section B - Applied Questions

Each question is worth 8 marks.

11. A client says, "We need an app because competitors have one." Explain how a BA should respond using Week 01 and Week 03 concepts.
12. A project combines strict regulatory approvals with uncertain user needs. Explain the BA planning trade-off and how governance should be handled.
13. A stakeholder workshop produces a long list of desired features. Explain how the BA should convert this into usable, prioritised requirements.
14. A future state is described in detailed vendor-specific terms before the current state has been analysed. Explain the risk and how the BA should correct the approach.
15. A solution meets the documented requirements but does not create expected business value. Explain how this could happen and how solution evaluation should respond.

## Section C - Scenario Analysis

Each question is worth 20 marks.

16. A national retailer receives growing customer complaints about late deliveries, inconsistent stock information, and poor communication between stores, warehouses, and online support. Executives want to buy a new order-management platform quickly.

Write an exam-style answer explaining how a digital business analyst should approach the situation from initial problem framing through current state analysis, stakeholder engagement, requirements, future state, change strategy, and solution evaluation.

17. A university has implemented a new student support portal. Ticket volumes have fallen, but many students now visit service desks in person because they do not trust the portal responses. Staff say the portal handles simple questions but fails on complex cases.

Using solution evaluation and earlier course concepts, explain how the BA should assess whether the portal is delivering value and what actions may be recommended.
