# Milestone Exam 03 - Strategy Design and Solution Evaluation

Course: [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Course Overview|Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)]]
Coverage: [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Week 08/Lecture|Week 08]], [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Week 09/Lecture|Week 09]], [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Week 10/Lecture|Week 10]], [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Week 11/Lecture|Week 11]]
Suggested timing: 90 minutes, closed book
Marks: 50

## Scope

This milestone exam checks current state analysis, future state analysis, business goals and objectives, solution scope, gap analysis, change strategy, requirements analysis and design definition, and solution evaluation.

## Section A - Short Answers

Each question is worth 2 marks.

1. Distinguish a business need from a business requirement.
2. Explain how POPIT can structure current state analysis.
3. Explain why personas should be evidence-based rather than invented stereotypes.
4. Explain the purpose of a customer journey map.
5. Distinguish future state from solution scope.
6. Distinguish solution scope from the final solution.
7. Explain how gap analysis links current state and future state.
8. Name four factors that should be considered in enterprise readiness assessment.
9. Distinguish requirement verification from requirement validation.
10. Distinguish a metric from a KPI.

## Section B - Applied Questions

Each question is worth 5 marks.

11. A team writes the objective "Improve customer service." Rewrite it as a stronger SMART objective and explain what makes it stronger.
12. A business wants to implement a chatbot immediately. Explain how current state analysis, personas, and journey mapping could test whether that solution fits the need.
13. A proposed solution meets functional requirements but users are unlikely to adopt it. Explain whether this is a solution limitation, enterprise limitation, or both.
14. A dashboard tracks the data that is easiest to collect, not the data tied to business goals. Explain the risk and how a BA should improve the metrics.

## Section C - Integrated Scenario

Each question is worth 10 marks.

15. A healthcare booking service wants to reduce missed appointments. Current problems include long phone queues, inconsistent reminders, low digital literacy for some patients, and poor integration between booking and clinician calendars.

Explain how a BA should conduct current state and future state analysis before recommending a solution. Refer to business need, personas, customer journey maps, SMART objectives, gap analysis, change strategy, and readiness.

16. A new online claims system has been implemented. Processing time has improved, but customer complaints have increased and staff say exception cases are harder to manage.

Using solution evaluation concepts, explain how the BA should measure performance, analyse the results, assess solution and enterprise limitations, and recommend actions to increase value.
