## Quiz 1

### Note-Based Questions

1. Which BACCO concept refers to "a problem or opportunity to be addressed"?
   - A. Context
   - B. Need
   - C. Solution
   - D. Value

2. Which BACCO concept refers to the people or groups affected by, involved in, or interested in a change?
   - A. Value
   - B. Stakeholder
   - C. Context
   - D. Solution

3. Why is business analysis described as more than a requirements documentation role?

4. Which item is not one of the BABOK knowledge areas listed in the Week 01 note?
   - A. Strategy analysis
   - B. Solution evaluation
   - C. Financial accounting
   - D. Requirements life cycle management

5. Explain how the BACCO concepts are interdependent.

6. According to the Week 01 note, what was a key early problem that helped create the need for business analysts?
   - A. Weak communication between business stakeholders and IT developers.
   - B. A shortage of software programming languages.
   - C. Too much business analysis maturity.
   - D. Complete agreement between business and IT teams.

7. What is the central task of business analysis before recommending a solution?

8. Which statement best describes the Week 01 note's view of BABOK knowledge areas?
   - A. They are a fixed sequence that must always be followed.
   - B. They are applied iteratively depending on the project context.
   - C. They only apply after solution implementation.
   - D. They remove the need for stakeholder engagement.

9. Name three competencies of a business analyst from the Week 01 note.

10. Why should digital technologies not be treated as solutions by default?

### Extension Questions

11. IIBA describes BACCM as a common language for effective business analysis. How could using a shared model such as BACCM reduce confusion in a cross-functional digital transformation team?

12. In IIBA's business analysis standard, business analysis is concerned with enabling change by defining needs and recommending solutions that deliver value. Which risk is most likely if a team skips the "defining needs" part?
   - A. The team may implement a technically working solution that does not solve the real business problem.
   - B. The team will necessarily deliver more stakeholder value.
   - C. The team will automatically reduce project governance requirements.
   - D. The team will remove the need for requirements lifecycle management.

13. Business-IT alignment research often treats alignment as an ongoing management concern, not a one-off project activity. How does this strengthen the case for business analysts having both business and technology knowledge?

14. Give one example of a digital initiative where the "solution" appears obvious at first, but a business analyst should still investigate the need, stakeholders, value, and context before recommending action.

15. Which statement best extends the Week 01 notes into professional practice?
   - A. A business analyst should only work after a technology solution has been selected.
   - B. A business analyst should help connect business needs, stakeholder value, technology capability, and organisational context before and during change.
   - C. Business analysis is useful only for software projects.
   - D. The main purpose of business analysis is to maximise documentation volume.
