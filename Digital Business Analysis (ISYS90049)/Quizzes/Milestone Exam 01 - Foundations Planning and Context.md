# Milestone Exam 01 - Foundations Planning and Context

Course: [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Course Overview|Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)]]
Coverage: [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Week 01/Lecture|Week 01]], [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Week 02/Lecture|Week 02]], [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Week 03/Lecture|Week 03]]
Suggested timing: 60 minutes, closed book
Marks: 40

## Scope

This milestone exam checks delayed recall and application of business analysis foundations, BA planning and monitoring, governance, performance improvement, and context analysis.

## Section A - Core Concepts

Answer each question briefly. Each question is worth 2 marks.

1. Explain why business analysis should begin with the business need rather than the assumed solution.
2. Name the six BACCO concepts and explain how changing one concept can affect the others.
3. Distinguish a business analysis task from a business analysis technique.
4. Explain why a BA plan is a living document.
5. Distinguish a predictive BA approach from an adaptive BA approach.
6. Explain the difference between BA performance improvement and final solution evaluation.
7. Distinguish PEST analysis from Porter's Five Forces.
8. Explain how SWOT combines internal and external context analysis.
9. Give two reasons why context analysis reduces the risk of recommending the wrong digital solution.
10. Name three BA competencies and explain why each matters in digital business analysis.

## Section B - Applied Distinctions

Each question is worth 5 marks.

11. A sponsor says, "We already know the solution is a customer portal, so the BA should just document requirements." Explain what is weak in this reasoning using BACCO language.
12. A BA team measures success by how quickly requirements documents are completed. Explain the risk in this measure and propose a better performance measure.
13. A project has strict approval requirements but changing user needs. Explain how the BA should balance governance and adaptability.
14. A retailer is considering a digital self-service system. Explain how the analyst could use PEST, Porter's Five Forces, and SWOT without turning the analysis into a generic checklist.

## Section C - Scenario

Answer in structured paragraphs. This question is worth 10 marks.

15. A medium-sized logistics company says it needs a new scheduling system because deliveries are often late. Managers blame outdated technology, drivers blame unclear route changes, and customer service staff say customers receive inconsistent updates.

Using Weeks 01-03, explain how a business analyst should frame the problem before recommending a solution. Your answer should refer to business need, stakeholders, context analysis, BA planning, governance, and evidence.
