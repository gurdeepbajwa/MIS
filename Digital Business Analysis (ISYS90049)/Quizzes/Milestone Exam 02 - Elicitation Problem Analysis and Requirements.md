# Milestone Exam 02 - Elicitation Problem Analysis and Requirements

Course: [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Course Overview|Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)]]
Coverage: [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Week 04/Lecture|Week 04]], [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Week 05/Lecture|Week 05]], [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Week 06/Lecture|Week 06]], [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Week 07/Lecture|Week 07]]
Suggested timing: 75 minutes, closed book
Marks: 45

## Scope

This milestone exam checks stakeholder engagement, scope, elicitation, problem analysis, root cause analysis, BA practice, and requirements life cycle management.

## Section A - Short Answers

Each question is worth 2 marks.

1. Explain why scope definition reduces the risk of scope creep.
2. Explain how POPIT helps avoid treating technology as the whole solution.
3. Distinguish stakeholder identification from stakeholder analysis.
4. Explain why elicitation should be prepared, conducted, confirmed, and communicated.
5. Distinguish planned elicitation from unplanned elicitation.
6. Explain why problem analysis should separate symptoms from causes.
7. Distinguish reactive and preventive problem analysis.
8. Explain how the Five-Why method can be useful and how it can be misused.
9. Name four qualities of a good requirement.
10. Distinguish functional requirements from non-functional requirements.

## Section B - Applied Questions

Each question is worth 5 marks.

11. A stakeholder with high influence but passive opposition is excluded from elicitation because the project team finds them difficult. Explain the likely risk and how the BA should respond.
12. A business user says, "The root cause is that the system is bad." Explain how a BA should test whether this is a cause, symptom, or premature solution framing.
13. A project has many requirements but limited delivery capacity. Explain how the BA should prioritise and manage requirements changes.
14. A stakeholder asks for a vague requirement: "The system must be easy to use." Rewrite it into a stronger requirement or explain what extra elicitation is needed.
15. Explain how Week 06's practice-oriented view of BA work connects ideation, problem analysis, option assessment, requirements, delivery support, and solution validation.

## Section C - Scenario

This question is worth 10 marks.

16. A university service team receives complaints that online enrolment is confusing. Students say instructions are unclear, staff say forms are incomplete, IT says the system follows the approved workflow, and managers want a quick fix before the next intake.

Using Weeks 04-07, explain how a BA should plan stakeholder engagement, choose elicitation techniques, define the problem, and convert findings into manageable requirements. Include risks if the team jumps straight to a system change.
