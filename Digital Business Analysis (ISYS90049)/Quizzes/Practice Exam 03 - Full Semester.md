# Practice Exam 03 - Full Semester

Course: [[Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)/Course Overview|Digital Business Analysis (ISYS90049)]]
Coverage: Weeks 01-12
Suggested timing: 2 hours, closed book
Marks: 100

## Instructions

Answer all questions. Prioritise applied reasoning, clear structure, and accurate distinctions between similar concepts.

## Section A - Short Answers

Each question is worth 2 marks.

1. What is the central task of business analysis before recommending a solution?
2. How does BA maturity change the analyst's role?
3. Distinguish decision-making governance from approvals.
4. Why should information management consider privacy and access rights?
5. How does Porter's Five Forces differ from SWOT?
6. What is the purpose of a stakeholder matrix?
7. Why might a workshop be better than individual interviews?
8. What is the purpose of a problem statement?
9. Distinguish transition requirements from functional requirements.
10. What does benchmarking add to solution evaluation?

## Section B - Applied Questions

Each question is worth 8 marks.

11. A project team defines success as "launch the system by August." Explain why this is not enough as a business objective.
12. A stakeholder requests a feature that conflicts with the agreed solution scope. Explain how the BA should assess the change.
13. A customer journey map shows pain points that were not mentioned by internal stakeholders. Explain how this should affect analysis.
14. A requirement is technically feasible but not aligned with business goals. Explain how verification and validation apply.
15. A solution's benefits appear after a delay. Explain how continuous monitoring should be designed.

## Section C - Scenario Analysis

Each question is worth 20 marks.

16. A private training provider wants to reduce student dropout. Leadership believes the answer is an AI chatbot. Tutors say students need better course guidance, support staff say data is fragmented, and students say reminders are generic and not useful.

Using the full course, explain how a BA should analyse the need, current state, stakeholders, requirements, future state, solution options, and evaluation measures before recommending action.

17. A digital claims platform has reduced staff workload but increased complaints from vulnerable users. The organisation wants to know whether to expand, redesign, or partially retire the solution.

Use solution evaluation, stakeholder analysis, metrics, enterprise limitations, and value-based recommendation concepts to explain how the BA should advise the organisation.
