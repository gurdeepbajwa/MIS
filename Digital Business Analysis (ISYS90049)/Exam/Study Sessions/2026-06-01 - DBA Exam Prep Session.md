## Session Focus

Hour 1: Exam map and section strategy.

The DBA exam is a 2-hour closed-book written examination. The current revision focus is building a strong mental map of the four main exam sections before drilling topic detail.

## Core Exam Map

| Section | Main focus |
| --- | --- |
| Planning | BA planning, business needs, context, risks, stakeholder access, governance, and elicitation planning. |
| Strategy analysis | Business need, current state, root causes, future state, constraints, gap analysis, and change strategy. |
| Requirements analysis and design | Requirement types, requirement quality, modelling, use cases, verification versus validation, and design options. |
| Solution evaluation | Measures, metrics, KPIs, solution limitations, enterprise limitations, and value realisation. |

## Core Answer Formula

Use this structure for most DBA exam answers:

1. Identify the business need or analysis problem.
2. Name the relevant BA concept, task, model, or technique.
3. Define it briefly.
4. Apply it to the case facts.
5. State the output the BA should produce.
6. Explain how the output supports value, decision quality, stakeholder alignment, or risk reduction.

### Q3

Question: Give two common exam traps for DBA planning questions.

User answer:

> Trying to provide a solution before a full analysis is done on the issue.  
> Not getting key stakeholders required to perform the change.

Mark: 1.75/2.0

Feedback: The first trap is strong: DBA planning questions often penalise jumping to solutions before the business need, scope, stakeholders, and analysis approach are understood. The second trap is also relevant, but tighten the wording: the issue is not just getting stakeholders to perform the change; it is ensuring the BA has access to the right stakeholders for elicitation, validation, governance, and scope decisions.

### Q4

Question: In DBA planning, what is the difference between a business need and a business requirement?

User answer:

> A business need is what the business is having problems with and want a solution for.  
> A business requirement is what the solution much achieve.

Mark: 0.75/1.0

Feedback: The distinction is mostly correct. A business need is the underlying problem, opportunity, or desired outcome that justifies change. A business requirement is a higher-level condition or capability the business needs to satisfy that need. Tighten the wording by avoiding "what the solution must achieve", because that can drift into solution requirements. Business requirements should stay solution-independent where possible.

## Stronger Model Answer

Planning determines how the BA work will be conducted, including scope, stakeholders, elicitation, governance, risks, and analysis activities. Strategy analysis examines the business need, current state, root causes, future state, constraints, gaps, and change strategy. Requirements analysis and design defines and evaluates the requirements and models needed for a solution that meets stakeholder and business needs. Solution evaluation measures whether the implemented or proposed solution delivers value using measures, metrics, KPIs, and analysis of solution and enterprise limitations.

## Planning Exam Traps

Common traps in DBA planning questions:

1. Jumping straight to the solution instead of explaining the BA planning work first.
2. Confusing solution risks with BA work risks.
3. Listing techniques without explaining why they fit the case.
4. Ignoring stakeholder access, governance, scope, time pressure, and elicitation constraints.
5. Treating the business problem as obvious instead of validating the business need.
