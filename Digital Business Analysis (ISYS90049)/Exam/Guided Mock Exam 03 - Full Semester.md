## Section 1 - Planning

Questions 1 and 2 are based on the following case.

SafeClaim Insurance provides home and contents insurance across Australia. The company has strong brand recognition, but recent customer surveys show falling satisfaction with claims handling. Customers say they submit the same documents multiple times, receive different advice from different teams, and cannot see the status of their claim online. Claims assessors use a legacy claims platform, shared spreadsheets, email inboxes, and paper inspection reports. Managers say urgent claims are difficult to prioritise because data is incomplete and responsibility is unclear. The executive team wants to improve claim transparency, operational efficiency, regulatory compliance, and customer trust. They have asked a Business Analyst to prepare a Business Analysis Plan within one month.

### Question 1 - 11 marks

Discuss THREE key challenges that the Business Analyst could encounter in preparing a good Business Analysis Plan for SafeClaim. (3 marks)

Discuss TWO key problems experienced by SafeClaim and TWO opportunities that SafeClaim can seize. In your discussion, explain why those problems and opportunities need to be addressed. (8 marks)

#### Question 1 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 02/Lecture|Week 02 Lecture]] - BA planning, planning aspects, complexity and risk, governance, information management.
- [[../Week 03/Lecture|Week 03 Lecture]] - business context, internal context, external context, SWOT, PEST.
- [[../Week 04/Lecture|Week 04 Lecture]] - stakeholders, stakeholder analysis, elicitation.

How to answer:

- Planning challenges should focus on the BA plan, such as limited time, many stakeholder groups, fragmented information, unclear decision rights, compliance sensitivity, and difficulty accessing claims evidence.
- Problems may include repeated document submission, inconsistent advice, no claim-status visibility, incomplete data, unclear responsibility, and poor prioritisation.
- Opportunities may include a claim-status portal, standardised document capture, integrated claims information, better prioritisation, and improved compliance reporting.
- Explain each point using customer trust, operational efficiency, risk reduction, and regulatory compliance.

### Question 2 - 14 marks

Discuss THREE techniques that would be useful for analysing the context of the SafeClaim problem. For each technique, explain how it would help the context analysis. (6 marks)

Propose FOUR key activities that the Business Analyst should plan to perform throughout the analysis work. For each activity, explain the importance of the activity and identify elicitation technique(s) likely to be useful. (8 marks)

#### Question 2 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 03/Lecture|Week 03 Lecture]] - PEST, SWOT, Five Forces, internal context, business capabilities.
- [[../Week 04/Lecture|Week 04 Lecture]] - POPIT, stakeholder engagement, elicitation.
- [[../Week 08/Lecture|Week 08 Lecture]] - current state analysis, personas, customer journey maps.

How to answer:

- Use context techniques that help the BA see competitive, regulatory, operational, stakeholder, and customer-experience dimensions.
- Suitable techniques include PEST, SWOT, stakeholder analysis, POPIT, process modelling, and customer journey mapping.
- Activities could include current state analysis, stakeholder interviews, claims journey mapping, document and compliance review, system/data assessment, requirements elicitation, and solution option evaluation.
- Pair activities with elicitation methods: interviews, workshops, observation, document analysis, survey feedback, data analysis, or review sessions.

## Section 2 - Strategy Analysis

### Question 3 - 12 marks

MedPath Diagnostics operates pathology collection centres and laboratory testing services. Patients currently book appointments through phone calls, a basic web form, and walk-in queues. Clinics report long waiting times, patients receive unclear updates about test availability, and staff manually reconcile appointment lists with laboratory capacity. A senior manager wants MedPath to build a patient mobile app immediately because competitors offer digital booking. The manager says the team already knows the requirements and that current state analysis will only delay the launch.

Define the business need and business requirement in this scenario. (2 marks)

How should the Business Analyst respond to the senior manager's desire to quickly develop a mobile app without analysing the current state? Include FOUR key arguments in your response. (10 marks)

#### Question 3 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 08/Lecture|Week 08 Lecture]] - current state, business needs, POPIT, journey maps.
- [[../Week 09/Lecture|Week 09 Lecture]] - business requirement, goals, SMART objectives, constraints, solution scope.
- [[../Week 03/Lecture|Week 03 Lecture]] - competition and external context.
- [[../Week 04/Lecture|Week 04 Lecture]] - stakeholders and elicitation.

How to answer:

- Do not define the need as "build a mobile app." Define the need around waiting times, unclear information, capacity mismatch, or patient experience.
- The business requirement should express the outcome needed at business level.
- Explain that current state analysis helps identify why waits and capacity issues occur, what systems and processes are involved, who is affected, and what constraints matter.
- Treat competitor pressure as context and the desired launch timing as a constraint.
- Show that focused analysis can be time-boxed while still preventing a poor solution.

### Question 4 - 13 marks

StyleBox is an online fashion retailer. Customer satisfaction with delivered products is high, and return rates are lower than industry averages. However, the fulfilment manager says the picking, packing, and dispatch process still has issues. Warehouse staff report frequent stock-location errors, customer service staff handle many "where is my order" queries, and finance says express delivery costs are rising faster than sales.

Assuming StyleBox already achieves high customer satisfaction with delivered products, how is it still possible that there are issues with the process? Suggest TWO potential issues. (5 marks)

Which technique would you use for identifying each of the two suggested issues, and why? (6 marks)

Once the issues have been identified, how should the Business Analyst prioritise which issues to address first? (2 marks)

#### Question 4 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 05/Lecture|Week 05 Lecture]] - root cause analysis, Five Whys, fishbone, Pareto.
- [[../Week 08/Lecture|Week 08 Lecture]] - current state and process analysis.
- [[../Week 11/Lecture|Week 11 Lecture]] - measures, metrics, performance variance.

How to answer:

- Explain that product satisfaction does not mean the fulfilment process is efficient, predictable, cost-effective, or scalable.
- Potential issues include stock-location errors, process rework, poor dispatch visibility, rising express-delivery costs, bottlenecks, or weak data accuracy.
- Match techniques to issues. Process modelling can expose bottlenecks and handoffs; Five Whys can investigate repeated location errors; fishbone can organise multiple causes; data analysis can identify cost drivers; Pareto can focus on the few causes creating most cost or delays.
- Prioritise using frequency, impact, cost, customer risk, strategic importance, and Pareto analysis.

## Section 3 - Requirements Analysis and Design

### Question 5 - 12 marks

HarbourHome is launching a digital maintenance request service for rental properties. Tenants should be able to lodge maintenance requests, attach photos, select urgency, receive updates, and book repair times. Property managers should be able to triage requests, assign tradespeople, approve quotes, and record completion. HarbourHome wants the service to be secure, easy to use, reliable, and available during after-hours emergencies.

Identify THREE functional requirements and THREE non-functional requirements for the HarbourHome maintenance service. For each requirement, briefly explain why it is critical to the success of the service. (12 marks)

#### Question 5 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 07/Lecture|Week 07 Lecture]] - functional and non-functional requirements, requirement quality.
- [[../Week 10/Lecture|Week 10 Lecture]] - requirements classification, specify and model requirements.
- [[../Week 04/Lecture|Week 04 Lecture]] - stakeholder needs and elicitation.

How to answer:

- Functional requirements describe tenant, property-manager, and tradesperson actions.
- Non-functional requirements describe performance, privacy, security, availability, reliability, usability, accessibility, and auditability.
- Avoid vague wording such as "easy" unless you make it measurable or explain the quality clearly.
- Justify each requirement in terms of tenant safety, service speed, staff efficiency, risk, compliance, or trust.

### Question 6 - 13 marks

LearnEdge Schools is implementing an AI-based learning analytics platform. The platform will identify students who may need support, recommend learning resources, and show progress dashboards to teachers and school leaders. As the Business Analyst, you are verifying the quality of the requirements before they are approved.

Provide THREE examples of a good requirement for this project. Briefly explain why each requirement is "good." (6 marks)

Even after the requirements have been verified, explain why a manager may still believe some requirements are not "right." (3 marks)

Identify TWO ethical or cultural problems that could arise from implementing the AI-based learning analytics platform. Briefly explain why each is problematic. (4 marks)

#### Question 6 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 07/Lecture|Week 07 Lecture]] - good requirement attributes and requirements lifecycle management.
- [[../Week 10/Lecture|Week 10 Lecture]] - verification and validation.
- [[../Week 11/Lecture|Week 11 Lecture]] - metric misuse, privacy, bias, and performance measurement cautions.

How to answer:

- Write requirements that are clear, atomic, testable, feasible, and understandable.
- Explain why each example is a good requirement by naming the attribute it demonstrates.
- In the verification versus validation part, distinguish "written correctly" from "right for the business need and stakeholders."
- Ethical risks include student privacy, algorithmic bias, labelling students unfairly, over-surveillance, lack of transparency, and teacher resistance.

## Section 4 - Solution Evaluation

### Question 7 - 12 marks

PayPath Bank has implemented a digital loan application workflow. The workflow allows customers to submit documents online, checks completeness, routes applications to credit teams, and sends status updates. Six months after implementation, average processing time has fallen, but customer complaints remain high. Some applicants say they do not understand why documents are rejected, and credit assessors say complex applications still require manual workarounds.

Discuss TWO solution evaluation tasks that would be useful in evaluating the workflow. In your discussion, explain why PayPath would benefit from focusing on those tasks to evaluate and improve the solution. (12 marks)

#### Question 7 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 11/Lecture|Week 11 Lecture]] - solution evaluation tasks, performance measures, solution limitations, enterprise limitations.
- [[../Week 05/Lecture|Week 05 Lecture]] - problem and root cause analysis.
- [[../Week 08/Lecture|Week 08 Lecture]] - current state, personas, journey maps.

How to answer:

- Choose two formal tasks such as analyse performance measures, assess solution limitations, assess enterprise limitations, or recommend actions to increase value.
- Apply each task to the apparent value gap: processing time improved, but complaints and manual workarounds remain.
- Consider whether the issue is inside the workflow, in staff process, in customer communication, or in enterprise policy.
- Explain how the task helps produce evidence-based recommendations.

### Question 8 - 13 marks

MealLoop is a meal-kit subscription company that has launched a recipe-personalisation tool. The tool recommends meal plans, adjusts ingredient quantities, and suggests substitutes for dietary preferences. Management wants to know whether the tool improves retention, reduces food waste, and improves customer satisfaction rather than simply increasing website clicks.

Define ONE possible measure, TWO possible metrics, and TWO KPIs that should be monitored once the tool is implemented. (9 marks)

Explain why you chose those two KPIs. (4 marks)

#### Question 8 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 11/Lecture|Week 11 Lecture]] - measures, metrics, KPIs, continuous monitoring, good metrics.
- [[../Week 09/Lecture|Week 09 Lecture]] - business objectives and expected value.
- [[../Week 10/Lecture|Week 10 Lecture]] - validation and value-based recommendation.

How to answer:

- Use raw measures such as number of subscribers, cancelled subscriptions, recipe ratings, wasted ingredients, or skipped deliveries.
- Metrics could include retention rate, churn rate, average recipe rating, food waste rate, or recommendation adoption rate.
- KPIs should align with strategic objectives such as retention, waste reduction, and satisfaction.
- Justify why the chosen KPIs indicate value better than simple activity metrics.
