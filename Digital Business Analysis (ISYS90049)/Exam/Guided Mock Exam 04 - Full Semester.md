## Section 1 - Planning

Questions 1 and 2 are based on the following case.

PeakLearn Training is a private vocational education provider with campuses in Melbourne, Geelong, and Ballarat. It has strong course completion results in some programs, but student enquiries and support requests have increased sharply. Students complain that they receive different advice from admissions, student services, and trainers. Trainers say they do not have a clear view of student attendance, assessment progress, or support requests. Administrative staff use separate enrolment, learning-management, payment, and email systems. Regional students say they struggle to access timely support outside business hours. The executive team wants to improve student retention, service consistency, operational efficiency, and regional access. They have asked a Business Analyst to prepare a Business Analysis Plan within one month.

### Question 1 - 11 marks

Discuss THREE key challenges that the Business Analyst could encounter in preparing a good Business Analysis Plan for PeakLearn. (3 marks)

Discuss TWO key problems experienced by PeakLearn and TWO opportunities that PeakLearn can seize. In your discussion, explain why those problems and opportunities need to be addressed. (8 marks)

#### Question 1 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 02/Lecture|Week 02 Lecture]] - planning aspects, complexity and risk, BA governance, information management.
- [[../Week 03/Lecture|Week 03 Lecture]] - business context, strategy, SWOT, PEST.
- [[../Week 04/Lecture|Week 04 Lecture]] - stakeholder analysis and elicitation.

How to answer:

- Planning challenges could include one-month time pressure, multiple campuses, many stakeholder groups, fragmented systems, unclear governance, student privacy, and difficulty defining scope.
- Problems should use case evidence: inconsistent advice, disconnected systems, poor visibility of student progress, staff workload, and limited regional support.
- Opportunities could include integrated student support information, improved online support, standardised advice, proactive retention interventions, and better service analytics.
- Explain why each point matters to retention, trust, efficiency, access, and decision quality.

### Question 2 - 14 marks

Discuss THREE techniques that would be useful for analysing the context of the PeakLearn problem. For each technique, explain how it would help the context analysis. (6 marks)

Propose FOUR key activities that the Business Analyst should plan to perform throughout the analysis work. For each activity, explain the importance of the activity and identify elicitation technique(s) likely to be useful. (8 marks)

#### Question 2 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 03/Lecture|Week 03 Lecture]] - PEST, SWOT, internal context, business capabilities.
- [[../Week 04/Lecture|Week 04 Lecture]] - POPIT, stakeholder engagement, elicitation techniques.
- [[../Week 08/Lecture|Week 08 Lecture]] - current state analysis, personas, customer journey maps.

How to answer:

- Choose context techniques that expose external pressure, internal capability, stakeholders, scope boundaries, and the student experience.
- Suitable techniques include PEST, SWOT, stakeholder analysis, POPIT, process modelling, and student journey mapping.
- Activities could include current state analysis, student feedback analysis, staff interviews, system and information assessment, support-process mapping, requirements elicitation, and solution option evaluation.
- Pair activities with interviews, workshops, surveys, document analysis, observation, data analysis, or journey-mapping sessions.

## Section 2 - Strategy Analysis

### Question 3 - 12 marks

QuickBite operates a chain of quick-service restaurants. Customers order in store, through delivery platforms, and through a basic website. Complaints have increased because order status is unclear, menu availability differs across channels, and customer support cannot easily trace delivery issues. A senior manager wants QuickBite to build a new customer app immediately because competitors have polished apps. The manager says current state analysis is unnecessary because the app features are obvious. The executive team wants a visible improvement before the summer trading period.

Define the business need and business requirement in this scenario. (2 marks)

How should the Business Analyst respond to the senior manager's desire to quickly develop a customer app without analysing the current state? Include FOUR key arguments in your response. (10 marks)

#### Question 3 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 08/Lecture|Week 08 Lecture]] - current state, business needs, POPIT, customer journey maps.
- [[../Week 09/Lecture|Week 09 Lecture]] - business requirements, goals, objectives, constraints, solution scope.
- [[../Week 03/Lecture|Week 03 Lecture]] - external context and competitive pressure.
- [[../Week 04/Lecture|Week 04 Lecture]] - stakeholder engagement and elicitation.

How to answer:

- Define the need around poor cross-channel ordering visibility, inconsistent menu availability, support traceability, or customer experience.
- Define the business requirement as the outcome the business needs, not the preferred app.
- Acknowledge the summer deadline as a constraint.
- Give four arguments for current state analysis: understand root need, map current channels and data, identify stakeholder pain points, avoid copying competitors blindly, define scope, and reduce rework.
- Use the case facts throughout.

### Question 4 - 13 marks

BuildPro supplies building materials to trade customers. Customers are satisfied with the quality of products, and repeat-order rates remain high. However, the operations manager says the quote-to-delivery process is still problematic. Sales staff often promise delivery dates before stock is confirmed, warehouse staff report urgent picking requests that disrupt normal work, and finance reports that transport costs are difficult to predict.

Assuming BuildPro already achieves high customer satisfaction with its products, how is it still possible that there are issues with the process? Suggest TWO potential issues. (5 marks)

Which technique would you use for identifying each of the two suggested issues, and why? (6 marks)

Once the issues have been identified, how should the Business Analyst prioritise which issues to address first? (2 marks)

#### Question 4 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 05/Lecture|Week 05 Lecture]] - problem analysis, Five Whys, fishbone diagram, Pareto analysis.
- [[../Week 08/Lecture|Week 08 Lecture]] - current state analysis and process models.
- [[../Week 11/Lecture|Week 11 Lecture]] - performance measures and variance.

How to answer:

- Explain that product quality and repeat orders do not prove process efficiency, reliability, cost effectiveness, or scalability.
- Potential issues include poor stock confirmation, unreliable delivery promises, urgent work disrupting warehouse flow, high transport cost variance, and weak process controls.
- Use process modelling for handoffs and bottlenecks, Five Whys for delivery promise failures, fishbone for transport-cost variation, data analysis for cost patterns, and Pareto for high-impact causes.
- Prioritise by customer impact, cost, frequency, operational risk, feasibility, and strategic alignment.

## Section 3 - Requirements Analysis and Design

### Question 5 - 12 marks

CareNow Clinics is planning a patient appointment and follow-up service. Patients should be able to book appointments, update personal details, complete pre-visit forms, receive reminders, view follow-up instructions, and request prescription repeats. Clinic staff should be able to manage schedules, review forms, send follow-up instructions, and flag urgent requests. CareNow wants the service to protect patient information, be accessible, and remain reliable during peak booking periods.

Identify THREE functional requirements and THREE non-functional requirements for the CareNow service. For each requirement, briefly explain why it is critical to the success of the service. (12 marks)

#### Question 5 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 07/Lecture|Week 07 Lecture]] - requirement types and good requirement attributes.
- [[../Week 10/Lecture|Week 10 Lecture]] - requirements classification, specify and model requirements, verification and validation.
- [[../Week 04/Lecture|Week 04 Lecture]] - stakeholder needs and elicitation.

How to answer:

- Functional requirements should describe what patients or staff must be able to do.
- Non-functional requirements should cover qualities such as privacy, security, performance, reliability, availability, accessibility, and usability.
- Write requirements clearly enough to be testable where possible.
- Explain why each requirement matters for care continuity, patient safety, staff efficiency, trust, compliance, or service access.

### Question 6 - 13 marks

RosterMax is implementing an AI-based rostering and workforce-planning system for a hospital network. The system will forecast staffing demand, suggest rosters, flag overtime risk, and provide managers with staffing analytics. As the Business Analyst, you are verifying the quality of the requirements before approval.

Provide THREE examples of a good requirement for this project. Briefly explain why each requirement is "good." (6 marks)

Even after the requirements have been verified, explain why a manager may still believe some requirements are not "right." (3 marks)

Identify TWO ethical or cultural problems that could arise from implementing the AI-based rostering and workforce-planning system. Briefly explain why each is problematic. (4 marks)

#### Question 6 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 07/Lecture|Week 07 Lecture]] - good requirement attributes and requirements lifecycle.
- [[../Week 10/Lecture|Week 10 Lecture]] - verification, validation, requirements architecture.
- [[../Week 11/Lecture|Week 11 Lecture]] - KPI cautions, privacy, surveillance, and worker measurement risks.

How to answer:

- Provide three actual requirements and explain why they are good.
- Use requirement quality attributes such as atomic, complete, consistent, concise, feasible, unambiguous, testable, prioritised, and understandable.
- Explain that verification checks requirement quality, while validation checks whether the requirement is the right one for the business need, users, constraints, and expected value.
- Ethical or cultural issues may include unfair roster recommendations, bias against certain staff groups, privacy, surveillance, lack of transparency, staff trust, and resistance from managers or clinicians.

## Section 4 - Solution Evaluation

### Question 7 - 12 marks

TutorLink has implemented a tutor-matching platform for a large tutoring company. The platform recommends tutors, automates booking, sends session reminders, and collects post-session feedback. Four months after implementation, booking volumes have increased, but parent complaints have also increased. Some students are matched with tutors who are available but not suitable for their learning needs, and tutors say the platform does not account for preparation time.

Discuss TWO solution evaluation tasks that would be useful in evaluating the platform. In your discussion, explain why TutorLink would benefit from focusing on those tasks to evaluate and improve the solution. (12 marks)

#### Question 7 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 11/Lecture|Week 11 Lecture]] - solution evaluation tasks, solution limitations, enterprise limitations, recommendations to increase value.
- [[../Week 05/Lecture|Week 05 Lecture]] - problem analysis and root cause analysis.
- [[../Week 08/Lecture|Week 08 Lecture]] - personas and customer journey maps.

How to answer:

- Choose two Week 11 solution evaluation tasks and apply them deeply.
- Measuring and analysing performance could compare booking volume, match quality, parent satisfaction, tutor workload, and learning outcomes.
- Assess solution limitations if the matching logic does not account for learning needs. Assess enterprise limitations if tutoring policies, tutor profiles, or preparation expectations are misaligned.
- Explain how the task would guide recommendations such as adjusting matching criteria, changing workload rules, improving tutor data, or revising processes.

### Question 8 - 13 marks

CivicPay is a council payment platform for rates, parking fines, permits, and community facility bookings. The council has added a new self-service payment assistant that helps users find the correct payment type, calculate fees, and complete transactions. Management wants to know whether the assistant improves payment completion, reduces contact-centre workload, and improves user confidence.

Define ONE possible measure, TWO possible metrics, and TWO KPIs that should be monitored once the assistant is implemented. (9 marks)

Explain why you chose those two KPIs. (4 marks)

#### Question 8 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 11/Lecture|Week 11 Lecture]] - measures, metrics, KPIs, good metrics, continuous monitoring.
- [[../Week 09/Lecture|Week 09 Lecture]] - objectives and expected business value.
- [[../Week 10/Lecture|Week 10 Lecture]] - validation and value-based recommendation.

How to answer:

- Raw measures could include number of payments, number of abandoned payments, number of support calls, number of successful assistant sessions, or number of user complaints.
- Metrics could include payment completion rate, abandonment rate, support deflection rate, average resolution time, or user satisfaction score.
- KPIs should directly connect to the council's objectives: payment completion, workload reduction, and user confidence.
- Explain why each KPI is strategically important and avoids focusing only on simple activity volume.
