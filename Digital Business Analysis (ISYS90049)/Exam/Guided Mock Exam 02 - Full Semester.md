## Section 1 - Planning

Questions 1 and 2 are based on the following case.

RiverCare Aged Services operates 22 residential aged-care homes and a growing home-care service across Victoria. RiverCare has a good reputation for compassionate care, but it is facing pressure from families, regulators, and competitors. Family members complain that updates about residents are delayed or inconsistent. Care staff record some notes on tablets, but incident reports, rostering, medication checks, and family communications are spread across different systems and paper forms. Managers say reporting for compliance audits takes too long because information must be collected manually from each site. The executive team wants to improve transparency, care coordination, compliance reporting, and family trust. They have asked a Business Analyst to prepare a Business Analysis Plan within one month.

### Question 1 - 11 marks

Discuss THREE key challenges that the Business Analyst could encounter in preparing a good Business Analysis Plan for RiverCare. (3 marks)

Discuss TWO key problems experienced by RiverCare and TWO opportunities that RiverCare can seize. In your discussion, explain why those problems and opportunities need to be addressed. (8 marks)

#### Question 1 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 02/Lecture|Week 02 Lecture]] - BA planning, planning aspects, complexity and risk, governance, information management.
- [[../Week 03/Lecture|Week 03 Lecture]] - external and internal context, SWOT, PEST, strategy and capabilities.
- [[../Week 04/Lecture|Week 04 Lecture]] - stakeholders, stakeholder analysis, elicitation techniques.

How to answer:

- Keep planning challenges separate from RiverCare's operational problems.
- Planning challenges may include time pressure, many sites, sensitive stakeholder access, regulatory complexity, fragmented information, and governance across clinical and executive decision makers.
- Problems should come directly from the case: delayed family updates, inconsistent records, manual audit reporting, disconnected systems, paper forms, or reduced trust.
- Opportunities could include integrated care records, better family communication, stronger audit readiness, improved information governance, and standardised workflows.
- Explain why each problem or opportunity matters for care quality, compliance, family confidence, staff workload, or long-term competitiveness.

### Question 2 - 14 marks

Discuss THREE techniques that would be useful for analysing the context of the RiverCare problem. For each technique, explain how it would help the context analysis. (6 marks)

Propose FOUR key activities that the Business Analyst should plan to perform throughout the analysis work. For each activity, explain the importance of the activity and identify elicitation technique(s) likely to be useful. (8 marks)

#### Question 2 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 03/Lecture|Week 03 Lecture]] - PEST, SWOT, internal context, strategy, business capabilities.
- [[../Week 04/Lecture|Week 04 Lecture]] - POPIT, stakeholder engagement, elicitation process and techniques.
- [[../Week 08/Lecture|Week 08 Lecture]] - current state analysis, personas, customer journey maps.

How to answer:

- Choose techniques that cover external regulation, internal operations, stakeholders, and process or information boundaries.
- Suitable techniques include PEST for regulatory and social pressure, SWOT for strategic position, stakeholder analysis for residents, families, staff and regulators, POPIT for scope, and journey mapping for family communication.
- For activities, focus on analysis work such as current state analysis, stakeholder engagement, compliance information review, process mapping, requirements elicitation, and solution option evaluation.
- Pair each activity with an elicitation technique such as interviews, observation, document analysis, workshops, surveys, or data analysis.
- A strong answer explains why the activity improves the analysis, not just what the BA will do.

## Section 2 - Strategy Analysis

### Question 3 - 12 marks

BookTrail is a national travel-booking company. Customers currently use the website, phone support, and email to manage bookings. Complaints have increased because customers receive inconsistent information about cancellation policies, support agents search multiple systems for booking details, and refund requests take weeks to resolve. A senior manager wants to immediately build a customer mobile app that copies competitor features. The manager argues that current state analysis is unnecessary because the problem is obvious: BookTrail needs a modern app before the next holiday season.

Define the business need and business requirement in this scenario. (2 marks)

How should the Business Analyst respond to the senior manager's desire to quickly develop a mobile app without analysing the current state? Include FOUR key arguments in your response. (10 marks)

#### Question 3 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 08/Lecture|Week 08 Lecture]] - business needs, current state, POPIT, customer journey maps.
- [[../Week 09/Lecture|Week 09 Lecture]] - business need, business requirement, goals, SMART objectives, constraints, solution scope.
- [[../Week 03/Lecture|Week 03 Lecture]] - competitive context and strategic fit.
- [[../Week 04/Lecture|Week 04 Lecture]] - stakeholder engagement and elicitation.

How to answer:

- Define the business need as the business-level problem or opportunity, not as "build an app."
- Define the business requirement as the high-level outcome needed to address the need, such as improved support consistency, faster refund resolution, or better booking self-service.
- Acknowledge the holiday-season deadline as a constraint.
- Use four arguments: avoid solving symptoms only, understand processes and systems, identify stakeholder needs, define realistic scope, reduce rework, and ensure the solution supports business value.
- Use case facts about policies, refunds, channels, agents, and inconsistent information.

### Question 4 - 13 marks

PrintWorks manufactures customised packaging for small businesses. Customer ratings are high because the final products look professional and usually arrive by the promised date. However, the operations director believes the design-to-production process has major issues. Designers complain that customers approve artwork late, production staff report frequent rework due to unclear specifications, and finance reports that some jobs consume far more labour than estimated.

Assuming PrintWorks already achieves high customer satisfaction, how is it still possible that there are issues with the process? Suggest TWO potential issues. (5 marks)

Which technique would you use for identifying each of the two suggested issues, and why? (6 marks)

Once the issues have been identified, how should the Business Analyst prioritise which issues to address first? (2 marks)

#### Question 4 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 05/Lecture|Week 05 Lecture]] - symptoms, causes, root cause analysis, Five-Why method, fishbone diagram, Pareto analysis.
- [[../Week 08/Lecture|Week 08 Lecture]] - current state analysis and process description.
- [[../Week 11/Lecture|Week 11 Lecture]] - measures, metrics, performance variance.

How to answer:

- Start by explaining that customer satisfaction does not prove internal efficiency or profitability.
- Potential issues include rework, unclear handoffs, late approvals, poor specification quality, inaccurate estimating, excessive labour cost, or weak process visibility.
- Match each issue with a technique: process modelling for workflow and handoffs, Five Whys for delayed approvals, fishbone for specification quality, data analysis for labour variance, or Pareto analysis for repeated high-impact defects.
- Prioritise by impact on cost, time, quality, customer risk, frequency, and strategic goals.

## Section 3 - Requirements Analysis and Design

### Question 5 - 12 marks

CampusCare University is planning an online student support portal. Students should be able to submit support requests, upload evidence, track request status, receive updates, and book appointments with advisers. Staff should be able to triage requests, assign them to specialist teams, and record outcomes. The university wants the service to be accessible, secure, reliable, and useful for students who need urgent support.

Identify THREE functional requirements and THREE non-functional requirements for the CampusCare support portal. For each requirement, briefly explain why it is critical to the success of the service. (12 marks)

#### Question 5 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 07/Lecture|Week 07 Lecture]] - requirement types, functional and non-functional requirements, good requirement attributes.
- [[../Week 10/Lecture|Week 10 Lecture]] - requirements classification, specify and model requirements, verify and validate requirements.
- [[../Week 04/Lecture|Week 04 Lecture]] - stakeholder needs and elicitation.

How to answer:

- Functional requirements should describe actions the portal must support, such as request submission, upload, triage, appointment booking, notifications, and staff assignment.
- Non-functional requirements should describe quality conditions, such as accessibility, privacy, performance, availability, security, reliability, and auditability.
- Write requirements in clear and testable language where possible.
- For each requirement, explain its importance for student experience, staff workload, risk management, service trust, or duty of care.

### Question 6 - 13 marks

HireWise is a recruitment agency implementing an AI-enabled candidate screening system. The system will rank applications, identify missing information, suggest interview shortlists, and produce dashboard reports for recruiters and client managers. As the Business Analyst, you are verifying the quality of the requirements before they are approved.

Provide THREE examples of a good requirement for this project. Briefly explain why each requirement is "good." (6 marks)

Even after the requirements have been verified, explain why a manager may still believe some requirements are not "right." (3 marks)

Identify TWO ethical or cultural problems that could arise from implementing the AI-enabled candidate screening system. Briefly explain why each is problematic. (4 marks)

#### Question 6 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 07/Lecture|Week 07 Lecture]] - good requirement attributes and requirements life cycle management.
- [[../Week 10/Lecture|Week 10 Lecture]] - verification, validation, requirements architecture, design options.
- [[../Week 11/Lecture|Week 11 Lecture]] - metrics cautions, bias, privacy and worker measurement risks.

How to answer:

- Write three actual requirements, not just three labels.
- Explain why each is good using attributes such as atomic, unambiguous, feasible, testable, prioritised, understandable, complete, and consistent.
- For verification versus validation, say that verified requirements can be well written but still not right for the business need, stakeholder needs, ethics, legal risk, or expected value.
- Ethical and cultural risks include algorithmic bias, unfair exclusion, privacy concerns, overreliance on AI, lack of transparency, candidate trust, and recruiter resistance.

## Section 4 - Solution Evaluation

### Question 7 - 12 marks

StockSure has implemented a stock forecasting dashboard for a chain of pharmacies. The dashboard predicts demand, recommends reorder quantities, and alerts store managers when products may run out. Two months after implementation, some stores have reduced excess stock, but other stores still experience shortages of high-demand items. Pharmacists say the dashboard does not always reflect local demand patterns, and head office says adoption varies widely across stores.

Discuss TWO solution evaluation tasks that would be useful in evaluating the dashboard. In your discussion, explain why StockSure would benefit from focusing on those tasks to evaluate and improve the solution. (12 marks)

#### Question 7 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 11/Lecture|Week 11 Lecture]] - solution evaluation tasks, performance measures, solution limitations, enterprise limitations, recommendations to increase value.
- [[../Week 05/Lecture|Week 05 Lecture]] - problem analysis and root cause analysis.
- [[../Week 08/Lecture|Week 08 Lecture]] - current state, stakeholders, and contextual variation.

How to answer:

- Pick two solution evaluation tasks from Week 11.
- Apply each task to the case. For example, measuring solution performance could compare stockouts, waste, forecast accuracy, and reorder compliance by store.
- Assess solution limitations if the prediction logic misses local demand. Assess enterprise limitations if staff adoption, training, process discipline, or store policies prevent value realisation.
- Explain what evidence is needed and how the task would lead to improvement actions.

### Question 8 - 13 marks

FitStudio has introduced a digital member-engagement platform for a chain of gyms. The platform supports class reminders, personalised workout suggestions, trainer messages, and membership-renewal prompts. Management wants to know whether the platform improves member retention and trainer productivity, not just whether members open messages.

Define ONE possible measure, TWO possible metrics, and TWO KPIs that should be monitored once the platform is implemented. (9 marks)

Explain why you chose those two KPIs. (4 marks)

#### Question 8 Guide

Lecture hints:

- [[../Week 11/Lecture|Week 11 Lecture]] - measures, metrics, KPIs, good metrics, continuous monitoring, cautions about KPIs.
- [[../Week 09/Lecture|Week 09 Lecture]] - goals, SMART objectives, expected value.
- [[../Week 10/Lecture|Week 10 Lecture]] - validation and value-based recommendations.

How to answer:

- Keep the distinction clear: measure is raw data, metric is interpreted or calculated, KPI is a strategically important metric.
- Align KPIs with retention and productivity, not only app activity.
- Suitable raw measures may include number of active members, number of renewals, number of trainer messages, or number of class bookings.
- Suitable metrics may include renewal rate, message response rate, class attendance rate, average trainer response time, or churn rate.
- Justify KPIs by linking them to business objectives and expected platform value.
